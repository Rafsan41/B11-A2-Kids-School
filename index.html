Globally, enrollment rates vary widely, but in many developed countries, over 90% of children aged 4-6 attend kindergarten. Studies show that children who participate in quality kindergarten programs are 20% more likely to excel in reading and math by third grade. Additionally, early education programs contribute to long-term success, with participants being 25% more likely to graduate high school. Kindergarten also significantly impacts social skills development, with 85% of teachers reporting noticeable improvements in peer interaction and emotional regulation among enrolled children.
